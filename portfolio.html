Week 8

This week we were given access to the Talking Economics and events and asked to make a note of one claim made and find some data to refute that.

On the Thursday, in the 'An Unfair Crisis' talk, Abi Adams-Prassl claimed that the labour market looked 'rosy' before the pandemic. Though large areas of the labour market were in a good condition, it is clear that the number of claimant counts was on the rise. When comparing this to the unemployment rate, which doesn't rise to nearly the same degree, it is clear that it is not just unemployed people that were claiming for assisstance in some form, but rather those that are working as well as claiming. This is could be due to the rise of workers in the gig economy, who may not be able to work enough to fully sustain themselves. Ultimately, this picture of the labour market does not look particularly 'rosy' as sustained growth on the claimant count - besides the obviosu spike from covid - could place extra pressures on government spending which means fewer jobs can be created from government spending. This could particularly be the case after covid as the pressures on spending will already be high.
